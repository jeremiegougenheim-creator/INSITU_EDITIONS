The story

Drawn for the Dragon Room.

In 2020, Yacine Bensalem was commissioned to design The Bankers Club, a 2,000-member private members club spanning 2,000 square meters in Hong Kong's central district. The Dragon Room — its inner sanctum, lacquered in auspicious red and gold, lined with brass inlays — needed seating that could hold the gravity of the room without competing with it.

Nothing on the market answered. We drew the chair you see here over four months, prototyped it twice with our manufacturing partner in the Pearl River Delta, and installed thirty-two units by spring 2021. Five years later, every single one is still in service. This is the production version of the chair that lives at the Bankers Club.

A chair has to hold the gravity of a room without competing with it. The Dragon was drawn for that.

The chinoiserie central motif — a brass medallion at the back, a botanical line drawn freehand — references the room's lacquered panels. The vermilion silk velvet was sourced from a mill in Lake Como that has supplied Hermès for forty years. The patinated oak frame is from an atelier two hours from our Hong Kong studio. We can be there in a morning.

Built for hospitality intensity. Finished for domestic comfort. Available in five upholsteries and three frame finishes. Six weeks production, then your choice of shipping.
